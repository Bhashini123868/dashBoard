Fruites

Fruits are generally considered a great source of essential vitamins like vitamin C, vitamin A (beta-carotene), and potassium, alongside dietary fiber, providing a low-calorie, nutrient-dense food option. Fruits are also known for their antioxidant properties, which help protect the body from oxidative stress and reduce the risk of chronic diseases such as heart disease and cancer.

Nutritional content: 10% - 20%

Bakery Items

When describing bakery items in terms of nutrition, key points to highlight include their often high carbohydrate content, typically from refined flour, alongside varying levels of added sugar and fat.

Nutritional content: 21%

Grains

Grains are important sources of many nutrients, including complex carbohydrates, dietary fiber, several B vitamins, and minerals (iron, magnesium, and selenium).

Nutritional content: 65%

Milk

Milk is a nutrient-rich beverage considered a significant source of protein, particularly casein and whey, along with essential minerals like calcium, phosphorus, and potassium.

Nutritional content: 4.9%

Fish

Fish is considered a highly nutritious food, providing a rich source of high-quality protein, essential omega-3 fatty acids, vitamins like vitamin D and B complex, and various minerals including selenium, iodine, phosphorus, calcium, iron, and zinc.

Nutritional content: 10% - 45%

Meat

When describing meat in relation to nutrition facts, it's important to highlight its high protein content, significant source of essential vitamins like B12, and minerals like iron and zinc.

Nutritional content: 1.2%

Vegetables

Vegetables are rich in vitamins, minerals, fiber, and antioxidants that promote health and prevent disease.

Nutritional content: 5% - 15%

Nuts

Nuts are packed with healthy fats, protein, and fiber, providing essential nutrients and energy.

Nutritional content: 50% - 60%

Legumes

Legumes are high in protein, fiber, and various essential vitamins and minerals, supporting heart and digestive health.

Nutritional content: 20% - 30%
